# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the ASvLLM community and its projects will be treated with respect and dignity.

As contributors and maintainers of this project, we are committed to fostering an open and welcoming environment that encourages:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards community members

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Be respectful**: Use inclusive language, respect differing perspectives
- **Be collaborative**: Work together constructively on problems
- **Be professional**: Keep discussions focused and productive
- **Be clear**: Communicate intentions clearly to avoid misunderstandings
- **Be supportive**: Help fellow community members learn and grow
- **Give credit**: Acknowledge others' ideas and contributions
- **Assume good intent**: Interpret others' words charitably

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment of any kind (discriminatory jokes, personal attacks, etc.)
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive comments
- Spam or self-promotion unrelated to the project
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Other conduct you would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clearly communicating standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that do not adhere to this code
- Temporarily or permanently banning community members for violations

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Public spaces where an individual represents the project or community
- Any conduct related to the ASvLLM project or its community

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue on GitHub:

**GitHub Issues**: [Report an issue](https://github.com/Arjunsilwal/ASvLLM/issues)

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter.

Project maintainers who do not follow this Code of Conduct may face temporary or permanent removal from decision-making roles.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or behavior deemed unprofessional.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the community for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any and all interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with those involved, including unsolicited interaction with maintainers, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

---

## Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal to the project maintainers with a detailed explanation. Appeals should be submitted within 30 days of the sanction. The maintainers will review and respond within 14 days.
